Our Why
What It Means to Be a Prisms Coach

Prisms coaches sit at the front line of a mission to break down barriers to access and build STEM proficiencies at scale, for every student, worldwide. Every visit, every Institute session, every district conversation is a chance to move that forward.

Being a coach means being a clear, energetic communicator who can upskill a teacher's confidence and practice through side-by-side co-teaching and real-time feedback. It means being a curriculum thinker who partners with district leaders to make Prisms fit the classroom, not the other way around. And it means being a community builder — fostering connection among the teachers and coaches in your region, and among each other.

What we hold each other to — Excellence, Velocity, Team, and Character. These are the culture and values every coach is evaluated against alongside measurable coaching impact.

Prisms Coaching Standards

Prisms Coaches Believe

  • We believe teachers are capable of being successful with Prisms.
  • We inspire, don't force.
  • We hold a high bar with authentic care.
  • We move with urgency in everything we do.
  • We push toward independence, every visit.
  • We do not blame. We do not make excuses. We elevate.

A Prisms Coaching visit should be felt — by the teacher, by the students, and by anyone in the room. Energy and presence are not optional. They are part of the standard.

The Non-Negotiables — present in every classroom, every day, every visit

  • Time is protected — a visible timer is set for every instructional segment; the 20-25 minute VR block is protected; synthesis time is protected.
  • The 4 core instructional routines run with fidelity — hardware routines, in-VR supports, synthesis discourse & writing routines, CFU revisions.
  • Student thinking dominates — synthesis discourse is actively facilitated; teachers use prompts that press for reasoning, revision, and risk-taking; feedback moves are visible; coaches protect productive struggle.
  • Coaching is explicit and moves toward independence — coach and teacher confirm the type of day (Tutorial, Co-Teaching, or Teacher-Led); roles and responsibilities are clear; a specific coaching objective is identified before instruction begins; live feedback is provided; teachers leave knowing exactly what to implement next.
Professional Expectations

Every interaction shapes a partnership. The way we communicate, prepare, collaborate, and show up in schools directly influences trust in our team and the long-term success of each partnership.

Professional Presence

Business professional or elevated business casual is required for all school-based coaching.

Acceptable: dress pants or tailored slacks, blouses/professional tops/collared shirts, sleeveless professional tops, button-down shirts, blazers or suit jackets, professional dresses or skirts, closed-toe flats/loafers/dress shoes/low professional heels or clean athletic sneakers.

Not appropriate: jeans (unless explicitly approved for a specific district day), ripped/distressed denim, athletic shorts, leggings worn as standalone pants, athletic tank tops, tank tops without a professional layer, sweatshirts or hoodies, casual athleisure wear, flip flops.

School Entry, Exit & Conduct

  • Arrive before scheduled coaching (30-45 min before the day begins; 10-15 min before a class period).
  • Sign in at the main office; be prepared with a government issued ID.
  • Greet administration and POC when possible; follow all school and safety visitor protocols.
  • Maintain confidentiality regarding students and staff; sign out at the main office.

In-Classroom Conduct

  • Model instructional excellence at all times.
  • Support teachers in establishing consistent instructional routines.
  • Maintain a calm, purposeful, student-centered tone; protect the full learning arc without allowing drift.
  • Leave classroom materials organized and returned.

Professional Communication

With teachers — solution-oriented and growth-focused; feedback is specific, actionable, and aligned to core routines; follow-up occurs within 24-48 hours of coaching visits.

With administration — maintain professional tone; reinforce consistent messaging around the implementation model; elevate successes and communicate challenges thoughtfully.

With students — respectful, encouraging, professional interactions; student-centered language aligned to Prisms instructional practices.

Email & written communication — clear subject lines, professional formatting, timely responses, proofread before sending.

Coaching Excellence & Admin Days

Coaching Days

Travel coaches typically spend 3 days/week in schools. Full-time district coaches typically spend 4 days/week in schools, with one Admin day. On school-based coaching days, coaches prepare for the specific lesson, align with the teacher on the visit's focus, protect the integrity of the instructional experience, coach toward teacher independence, capture implementation evidence, and follow up promptly.

Admin / Non-School Days — Priorities

  • Prepare for upcoming coaching (modules, lesson study, coaching moves).
  • Maintain proactive communication with teachers and district partners.
  • Use implementation data to plan the highest-leverage next steps.
  • Advance implementation projects and professional learning.

Admin days are active, connected workdays — coaches remain reachable, monitor Slack and email, and communicate proactively if stepping away.

Teacher Communication & Follow-Up

  • Send pre-coaching emails 48 hours before upcoming visits; confirm the lesson/module and align on goals.
  • Send post-coaching follow-ups within 24-48 hours with specific strengths observed and 1-2 actionable next steps.
  • Review and respond to all teacher communication within 24 hours on workdays.

Team Projects & Continuous Improvement

Coaches should not independently create new materials — all resource development must be discussed and aligned to PL team priorities (this Coach Hub site included).

Operational Expectations

Out of Office

Notify your manager and the appropriate CSM via Slack or email with dates. Once approved, add a calendar "Event" (not the automatic "OOO" type) named "Your Name OOO," set to Free, on the Coach Team OOO calendar — and update the PD Calendar. Update your Slack status to "Vacationing" or "OOO" for the days you're out.

Emergencies

Sick time — notify your manager, the CSM, and impacted school contacts immediately; update calendar/Slack; reschedule affected visits promptly.

Running late — notify all relevant parties immediately with an updated arrival time; communicate proactively rather than reactively.

Virtual Meeting Norms

  • Cameras on for all internal and external virtual meetings unless previously communicated otherwise.
  • Join on time — message attendees if running more than 5 minutes late.
  • Minimize distractions and multitasking; use professional or blurred backgrounds; wear appropriate attire.

Communication

  • Slack is our primary source of communication — internal Slack and email responses are expected the same day.
  • Coaches are expected to maintain accurate, visible Google Calendars reflecting working hours, coaching days, travel, and PTO.
Performance & Growth

Coaching excellence is developed over time through reflection, feedback, collaboration, and continuous learning.

Goal Setting & Alignment

Each coach identifies 1-2 personal growth goals aligned to coaching excellence, successful implementation, teacher independence, and team priorities — with bi-weekly manager check-ins to track progress.

Annual Performance Review

Conducted annually in the fall, reflecting two core areas: Measurable Goals (coaching day feedback/survey results, content module utilization, CFU implementation) and Culture & Values (Excellence, Velocity, Team, Character).

Ongoing Feedback

Throughout the year: observation of coaching work, bi-weekly 1:1s, collaborative reflection conversations, internal meeting participation, and review of coaching artifacts.
